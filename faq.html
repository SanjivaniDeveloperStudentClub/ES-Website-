Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How to know if your phone is hacked?

There are several signs that might indicate your phone is hacked:

  • Unusual battery drain.
  • Slow performance.
  • High data usage.
  • Unknown apps installed.
  • Strange pop-ups or ads.
  • Unusual activity on accounts linked to your phone.
What to do after your phone is hacked?

If you suspect your phone has been hacked, follow these steps:

  1. Remove any unknown apps.
  2. Run a security scan using a trusted antivirus app.
  3. Change passwords for your accounts, especially those linked to your phone.
  4. Enable two-factor authentication for additional security.
  5. Factory reset your phone if the issue persists (make sure to back up your data first).
  6. Contact your phone provider for further assistance.
How do I create strong passwords and manage them securely?

Creating strong passwords and managing them securely is essential for online security:

  • Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or names.
  • Consider using a password manager to generate and store complex passwords.
  • Regularly update passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where possible.
  • Be cautious of phishing attempts and always verify websites before entering passwords.
How do I report phishing emails or websites?

If you receive a suspicious email or come across a suspicious website, follow these steps:

  1. Do not click on any links or download attachments.
  2. Mark the email as spam or phishing if your email provider offers this option.
  3. Report phishing emails to the relevant authorities or your company's IT department.
  4. If the phishing attempt is related to a financial institution, contact the institution directly using a trusted contact method.
  5. Regularly update passwords and enable multi-factor authentication where possible.
  6. Be cautious of phishing attempts and always verify websites before entering passwords.
